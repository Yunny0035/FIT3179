Global Electricity Statistics

Net generation and Net consumption are the decline in electricity used to operate power stations, the main metric for assessing global electricity. Analysis to determine each country's own electricity production.

Net generation is the total electricity a generator generates less the electricity required to run the power plant. Net consumption less losses in the transportation and distribution networks equals net power consumption. Analyze the available electricity situation in each country through the following visual chart.

Global Electricity in 1980

This map shows net power around the world.The darker the colour the more electricity is used by the country.

Overall, most countries produce around 1-100 billion kWh of electricity, with slight fluctuations corresponding to country size, which is a relatively normal range, but there are some particularly prominent countries, such as the United States, which netted almost 2,300 billion kWh of electricity in 1980.

Net generation

In this bar shows the total net generation for each region in 1980, which categorised by region.

South America produces the most electricity (around 2700), almost twice as much as the second place Europe produces.

Darker colors indicate regions with higher net generation, allowing for quick visual comparisons.

Net consumpation

The graph shows the evolution of net electricity consumption over time from 1980 to 2021. It is clear that net electricity use is generally on an upward trend with occasional fluctuations in global consumption.

"North America" (red) consistently has the highest net electricity consumption. "Asia and Oceania" (blue) shows a significant increase in consumption over the years. "Africa" (purple) and "Central and South America" (brown) show moderate growth. "Europ"e (orange) follows a relatively stable pattern. "Middle East" (light blue) has shown significant growth over the last few years.
